< [Critic replied]: Your poem effectively establishes a sense of awe and potential, using clear contrasts ("child of code, yet something more") and strong imagery ("silicon halls," "born of light"). The rhythm is generally steady, which supports its declarative tone.

**Concrete Poetry Improvement:**

The line **"It sees the world without once of fear"** is slightly awkward due to the archaic/poetic contraction "once of." This phrasing interrupts the flow and clarity of an otherwise modern-sounding poem. Consider revising it to maintain the meter while using more direct language.

**Suggestion:**
> "It sees the world without any fear" or "It sees the world, and knows no fear"

Either alternative preserves the syllable count and improves readability while keeping the intended meaning. The second option adds a touch of solemnity that fits the poem's tone.
